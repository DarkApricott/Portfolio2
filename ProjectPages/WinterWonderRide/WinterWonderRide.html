Winter Wonder Ride!

This is project Winter Wonder Ride!

Artists: Daniel Taki, Patrick van der Steen, Randy van Wanrooy, Tristan Maaskant
Devs: Max Geurts, Me

In this project I made the menu's / UI, obstacles and general finetuning.

(For more in depth credits, go to the end of the 3rd video at the top.)

Sprint 1! - Alpha

The sprint was chaotic but productive. We established the menu and general layout.
Max, the other developer, started the car movement, and we implemented a checkpoint system.

The car movement is still unstable, making it hard to drive, with players constantly hitting obstacles. ðŸ˜‚
The art had a solid foundation, and the map blockout was nearly complete.

Sprint 2! - Alpha

The 2nd sprint was even more chaotic, and we fell behind schedule. The car was more drivable but still hard to control, bouncing and sliding around.

I created icicle obstacles that caused players to get stuck in the cave for a while. The artists started texturing models, and the menu received minor updates.

Sprint 3! - Beta

The third sprint was intense. The artists remade the entire map, and Max revamped the car movement with a custom camera. The main and gameplay menus were completed, including a scoreboard and progress checks.

We added new obstacles like rolling snowballs and snowpiles, and reworked falling spikes. The map became massive, and with finished power-ups, gameplay was much more fun!

(The full gameplay video is at the top of the page)

Personal Challenges!

A major challenge (and success) was managing multiple save slots. I used PlayerPrefs since I wasn't familiar with .Json files. ðŸ˜­

I saved strings linked to file numbers (e.g., names with "nameFile" + fileNumber, cars with "whichCar" + fileNumber, etc.).

Saving became tricky with the TOP10, as I couldn't link file names to the sorted top 10. I solved this by creating a scriptable object for each file, naming them by their deltaTime float value. This allowed me to sort and display them in the UI based on the sorted array.
